Distilled Spirits Labeling

Distilled spirits must be properly labeled before it can be sold in the U.S. marketplace. This guidance will help you understand TTB’s requirements for distilled spirits labels.

Mandatory Distilled Spirits Label Information
An explanation of each statement required to appear on most distilled spirits labels
Required information on a labelRequired information on any label
  • Brand Name
  • Class or Type Designation (the kind of distilled spirits based on the standards of identity)
  • Alcohol Content
  • Age Statement
  • Color Ingredient Disclosures (if applicable)
  • Commodity Statement (if applicable)
  • Health Warning Statement
  • Name and Address
  • Net Contents
  • Country of Origin (imports only)

Designing your label? Use this Mandatory Information Checklist prior to applying for label approval.

NOTE: Want a concise guide to mandatory labeling information? View the Distilled Spirits Beverage Alcohol Manual.

Optional DS Label Information
Guidance about labeling distilled spirits with this optional information
Most commonly usedDietary information
  • Distinctive or Fanciful Name 
    (mandatory for specialty products and optional for other spirits)
  • Alcohol Content 
    (may be expressed in degrees of proof in addition to the required alcohol by volume statement)
  • Gluten Content Statements
  • Major Food Allergen Labeling
  • Statements Related to Nutrient Content:
    • Serving Facts
    • Alcohol Facts, and
    • Sugar Content
Production claimsMiscellaneous
  • Organic Claims
  • Personalized Labels
